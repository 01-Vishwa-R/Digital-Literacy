Responsible Social Media: Do’s

-> Check your privacy settings regularly
-> Make sure you know who can see your posts, photos, and personal details. It’s easy to forget these settings over time.
-> Keep your profile professional
-> Especially on platforms like LinkedIn, update your profile with your projects, skills, and achievements so it reflects your growth.
-> Think before you post
-> If something feels emotional or controversial, take a moment before posting. A small pause can prevent unnecessary problems.
-> Verify information before sharing
-> Not everything online is true. Always check the source before reposting news or content.
-> Follow meaningful content
-> Try to follow pages and people who add value—whether it’s learning, motivation, or career-related content.


Responsible Social Media: Don’ts

-> Don’t share sensitive personal information
-> Avoid posting things like your exact location, home address, or any private details.
-> Don’t post inappropriate or illegal content
-> Anything you share online can stay there permanently and affect your reputation.
-> Avoid posting negative rants
-> Complaining publicly about professors, classmates, or workplaces can create a bad impression.
-> Don’t fall for viral negativity
-> Some content is designed just to provoke reactions. It’s better not to engage with such posts.
-> Don’t compare yourself to others online
-> Social media often shows only the best moments of people’s lives. Don’t let that affect how you see yourself.
