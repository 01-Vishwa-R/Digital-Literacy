Rohit, a first-year engineering student, had been actively applying for internships. One evening, he received an email that appeared to be from a well-known tech company. The email congratulated him for being shortlisted and asked him to complete a quick verification process within 12 hours to secure his position.

Excited by the opportunity, Rohit didn’t think much and clicked on the link provided. The website looked professional and asked him to log in using his email credentials. Since everything looked genuine, he entered his details and submitted the form.

A few hours later, Rohit started receiving notifications about unusual login attempts on his email account. Soon after, he was locked out of his account completely. The attacker had gained access and began using his email to send similar phishing messages to others.

When Rohit reviewed the original email carefully, he noticed small details he had ignored earlier. The sender’s email address had a slight variation from the official domain, and the link redirected to a fake website.

This incident made Rohit realize how easily phishing attacks can trick users, especially when they create urgency and appear legitimate. It also showed the importance of verifying sources before sharing sensitive information online.
